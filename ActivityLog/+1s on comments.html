+1'd comment on post by Brian Rose+Chris Weber My issue with the use of red is only secondarily the fact that I find it to be a hideously jarring color.  My primary problem with it is that it is a hideously jarring color that I have no choice in.  The old g+ was in a muted color palette, that some people might have found boring, but at least wasn't an assault on the eyes. I am not saying that google needs to undo the red.  I am saying that they need to give us a choice. As for the sort, you and I appear to be seeing vastly different things, and since google doesn't give us a sort function that we can change, that is very odd. For me, under the classic view, posts were always in a pretty good approximation of chronological order.  I could start at the top and scan down, and once I had seen one or two posts that I was already familiar with I new that I was in "old" territory and I could stop scrolling.  Under the new mess, posts appear randomized with a slight chronological bias.  I have stuff 45min old buried under things a day and a half old, with no discernible reason why.  Even worse than that, I have posts I have seen appearing above posts that I haven't seen, meaning that I have no idea where the line of demarcation between "Old" and "New" lies. Both of them will re-shuffle if you refresh, but the old is a slight rearranging, while the new appears to be a full shuffle. If there is only going to be one choice, then that choice should be the most logical and utilitarian.  In this case, a strict chronological sort (withing the confines of a multi-server environment) If anything less utilitarian than that is going to be offered, then it needs to be offered as an option above and beyond the utilitarian.  — Links and images in g+ comments?
