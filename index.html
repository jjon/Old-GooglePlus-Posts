My notes on the results of Google's "Takeout" archiving service

When I got booted out of my old Google account (long story) I was able to migrate everything that mattered to my new Google account, except for my Google+ posts. There weren't many of them, but the lion's share of them were notices of "Music I Like", a collection that a few (maybe 3) people followed. I didn't want to lose these so I used Google's "Takeout" to create an archive. The result was an archive of html files. With very little tinkering, I was able to dump the whole archive into github's gh-pages, and this is the result. Of the directories listed below "Posts" is the one that has the posts (...huh). It included everything indiscriminately in chronological order. Maybe, at some future point, if I get bored, I'll scrape these into a blog or something and make them pretty. Now, not.
